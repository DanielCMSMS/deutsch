Modal Verbs

Modal verbs in English add more detail to verbs in a sentence. The words can, must, and would are examples of modal verbs:

I am eating lunch.
I can eat lunch.

I am studying for the test.
I must study for the test.

Like in English, modal verbs exist in German. Some examples are the words können, müssen, sollen, wollen, and dürfen. There are key differences on how modals are used in German.

The first is that a model verb is conjugated and used like any other verb, but the regular verb that it modifies is placed at the end of the sentence in the infinitive.

Ich kann meine Hausaufgabe machen.
Translation: I can do my homework.

In the above sentence, können is conjugated (irregularly) for the first person and placed like any other verb. However, machen stays at the end of the sentence.
